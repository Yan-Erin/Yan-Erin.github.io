Optimal transport entails finding the most efficient way to allocate a limited amount of resources. However, the most efficient transport plan may not always lead to a fair allocation of resources.

In this research project with Google Research, my group and I incorporated a fairness factor into the optimal transport plan. Using the alternating method of multipliers, we developed a distributed algorithm that negotiates solutions based on each pair of source and target without a need for a central planner. The algorithm yields fair and efficient results which are analyzed through our case studies.
